Bull Call Spread

Test a symmetric ITM/OTM call spread with third-Friday expirations.

This page uses a stock-only approximation for a one-lot bull call spread. For an entry stock price of $400 and a spread width of $40, the modeled position buys the $380 call and sells the $420 call, pays a $20 debit, and can settle at up to $40 if the stock finishes at or above $420. Net max profit is therefore $20 per share, or $2,000 per one-contract spread.

The model assumes the long ITM and short OTM calls have roughly offsetting extrinsic value. Debit is approximated as half the spread width, payoff at expiration is based on intrinsic value only, and a flat stock near the entry price is therefore modeled as roughly breakeven.
Each cycle opens one spread centered on a rounded-down strike anchor. For higher-priced stocks the center uses the nearest lower level ending in 0 or 5, and for lower-priced stocks it uses the nearest lower whole number. Lower strike = center minus half the width, upper strike = center plus half the width, debit = half the width, max value = full width.
